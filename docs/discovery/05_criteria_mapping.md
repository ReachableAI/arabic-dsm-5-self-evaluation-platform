## Mapping Principles

### 1. Simplify Clinical Language
- Replace medical jargon with everyday Arabic
- Use concrete examples instead of abstract criteria
- Ask about observable behaviours, not internal states

### 2. Frequency-Based Responses
- Standard 5-point scale for most questions
- Consistent response anchors across categories
- Time-referenced where criteria specify duration

### 3. Safe Framing
- Never imply diagnosis
- Questions are "reflection prompts"
- Gentle, supportive tone throughout

### 4. Criteria Fidelity
- Map each criterion to at least one question
- Preserve diagnostic intent without clinical terminology
- Flag criteria unsuitable for self-report
